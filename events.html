Hatsumode (First Shrine Visit of the New Year)

On New Year's Eve, and on the initial three days of the New Year (1, 2 and 3 January), Kyotoites visit either their nearby area hallowed place or one of Kyoto's significant sanctuaries to pay for karma, achievement and wellbeing in the New Year.

Toka Ebisu

This is one of our favorite Kyoto festivals. It’s held at Ebisu-jinja Shrine, which is a short walk from either Gion-Shijo or Kiyomizu-Gojo Stations on the Keihan Line. It happens all day from the 8th to the 12th, but it’s best to go in the evenings

Setsubun

Setsubun marks the start of spring as indicated by the old lunar schedule in Japan. Since it denotes a fresh start, it's the chance to standing off old terrible things and welcome in great new things.

Higashiyama Hanatoro

Higashiyama's Hanatoro is one of the cooler occasions of the Kyoto year. It's held in the Southern Higashiyama touring locale. Lamps are set along the paths and streets of the space,The impact is totally supernatural.

Kamogawa Odori Geisha Dance

During most of the month of May, the girls of the Pontocho geisha district perform the lovely and quaint Kamogawa Odori dance in the Pontocho Kaburenjo hall, overlooking the eponymous Kamo-gawa River.

Gozan no Okuribi (Daimonji Fire Festival)

This is the highlight of the month of August. More commonly known as the “Daimonji Fire Festival,” this is when they set those massive Chinese characters on the hills around town ablaze.

Jidai Matsuri

October 22nd is probably the greatest days of the year for Kyoto celebrations: In the daytime, you can get this occasion, and afterward head north in the evening to look at the Kurama-no-himatsuri.The Jidai Matsuri, which signifies "Celebration of the Ages", includes a motorcade of individuals wearing outfits from every one of the significant Japanese authentic periods. .

Fire Festival

This is arguably the most exciting festival of the year in Kyoto. It’s held in the village of Kurama, about 30 minutes by train from Kyoto. Teams of shouting men carry huge flaming torches through the narrow streets of this tiny mountain village. The atmosphere is positively primeval. .

Higashiyama Temple Illuminations

For all of November, to coincide with the fall foliage, one or more of the major temples in the Southern Higashiyama District hold special evening temple illuminations, in which the buildings and gardens are illuminated.

Arashiyama Hanatoro

This is a magical event: thousands of lanterns are placed along the lanes of Arashiyama, including the walkway through the famed Arashiyama Bamboo Grove. The effect is otherworldly. And you’ll find beautiful ikebana (flower arrangement) installations scattered about. If you’re in town, don’t miss it!.

Joya-no-Kane

In the wake of devouring New Year's Eve, numerous Kyotoites head to their neighborhood sanctuary to perform joya-no-kane, a hallowed ringer ringing function. Here, individuals line up to ring the monster sanctuary chimes and wish for favorable luck in the New Year (and to dispose of awful karma from the old).
